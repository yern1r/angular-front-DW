Буын - Syllable.

A syllable is a vowel sound or several sounds in a word that are pronounced in one breath. Syllables consisting of two or more sounds can end either on a vowel (open syllables: а-на - mother, а-та - father) or on consonants (semi-closed syllables: от - fire, өрт - fire), or both the beginning and end of the syllable can be closed with consonants (closed syllables: тас - stone, кен - ore).

The main rule of syllabication is as follows: If there is one consonant between vowels at the syllable boundary, it belongs to the next syllable (ә-ке, ә-же). If there are two consonants at the syllable boundary, the first belongs to the previous syllable, and the second to the next (ән - дер - мен - with songs).

Екпін - Stress.
Stress in the Kazakh language, compared to Russian, is more consistent, falling on one specific syllable of the word, usually the last. If affixes are added to words, the stress shifts to the last syllables of the affixes.

Буын үндестігі - Unified Law of Harmony

In Kazakh language, two types of harmony are distinguished:

a) harmony of vowel sounds;
b) harmony of consonant sounds.

Vowel Harmony

The essence of the law of harmony is as follows: depending on the root vowel, the word can only take either hard or soft affixes, such as -ler, -lık, -shy-lar-gha.

If the last syllable of the word is hard, then a hard affix is ​​attached to it. If the last syllable of the word is soft, then a soft affix is attached to it.

Consonant Harmony

Progressive and regressive assimilation

Consonant harmony comes in two forms: progressive and regressive assimilation. According to progressive assimilation: the last sound of the base influences the first sound of the ending. If the last sound is voiceless or voiced b, v, g, d, then the initial sound of the affix is voiceless.

For example: kitap tar, Ivanov tar.

If the last sound of the base is voiced, sonorant, or vowel, then the initial consonant sound of the affix is voiced or sonorant.

For example: aqyn dar, uy-diń, qyz-gha.

Subsequent Implementation

Regressive assimilation

If endings with initial voiceless consonants q, k, p are added to roots or bases with final voiceless consonants q, k, p and initial vowels ы or і, then the final voiceless consonants q, k, p become voiced sounds ǵ, g, b.

For example: qasyq+ı = qasyǵı, dop+ım = dobım.

Formation of the possessive form from nouns tap (class), ayaq (leg), júrek (heart) is as follows: jumısşı tabı (working class), stoldyń ayağı (table leg), jumystyń ayağı (end of work), adamnyń júregi (person's heart), etc.
